Engineering Research

Effects of a Thermal Gradient Airfoil in a Low Speed Wind Tunnel

Preliminary sketches

Assembly prototype

Teammate operating low speed wind tunnel
 A common job for an aeronautical engineer is match airfoils to specific applications. There are drawbacks to traditional wings, where mechanical devices add complexity. One possible modification to airfoil performance is a dempterature differential. I worked with a small team to design, build and test an airfoil with a temperature differential between the upper and lower surfaces. Utilizing Peltier-Devices, the airfoil could create a differential using electricity. The results of the change in coefficient of lift and drag are explained in the report. paper posting soon

CoinSentry LLC (mining and research)

Hardware research from purchasing decision making

Data for tuning GPU voltages

The two node racks for 16GPUs, test bench in background

 A hand-built group of supercomputer slave nodes managed by a host remote computer. Primarily used to mine PoW cryptocurrencies from 2013 to 2014. I co-founded a company with two objectives, and this was one half. The other half was a set of cryptocurrency alerts based on a web and desktop client. I created a stripped down linux to fit on flashdrives, and tuned the gpu voltages and clocks to optimize heat and efficiency. The motherboards would support up to 5 cards but for PSU efficiency, 4 HD7850s were the limit each. These things run hot!
