Effects of a Thermal Gradient Airfoil in a Low Speed Wind Tunnel

Preliminary sketches

Assembly prototype

Teammate operating low speed wind tunnel
 A common job for an aeronautical engineer is match airfoils to specific applications. There are drawbacks to traditional wings, where mechanical devices add complexity. One possible modification to airfoil performance is a dempterature differential. I worked with a small team to design, build and test an airfoil with a temperature differential between the upper and lower surfaces. Utilizing Peltier-Devices, the airfoil could create a differential using electricity. The results of the change in coefficient of lift and drag are explained in the report.
